## 1. METRIGON® (metrigonide) — oral tablet
- **Class/Indication**: once-daily oral agent for type 2 diabetes mellitus, adjunct to diet/exercise.
- **Dosing**: 25 mg once daily with breakfast; may titrate to 50 mg after 4 weeks. Renal: eGFR 30–59 → max 25 mg; eGFR <30 → not recommended. **No dose adjustment for mild hepatic impairment.**
- **Key warnings**: urinary tract infections, volume depletion/hypotension in elderly.
- **Common AEs**: UTI (7%), headache (5%), dizziness (3%).
- **Missed dose**: take when remembered same day; never double.

## 2. CORVALEX® (corvalexan) — oral tablet
- **Class/Indication**: heart-failure agent (HFrEF) reducing CV death/hospitalization.
- **Dosing**: 10 mg BID, titrate to 20 mg BID at 2 weeks as tolerated.
- **Key warnings**: **symptomatic hypotension and dizziness, esp. during first 4 weeks or with diuretics**; hyperkalemia — check K⁺ at baseline and 2 weeks; angioedema (rare) — discontinue permanently.
- **Common AEs**: dizziness (9%), hypotension (6%), hyperkalemia (4%), cough (2%).
- **Interactions**: avoid with potassium-sparing diuretics unless K⁺ monitored.

## 3. LUMENZA® (lumenzumab) — subcutaneous autoinjector (combination product)
- **Class/Indication**: IL-23 inhibitor biologic for moderate-to-severe plaque psoriasis, adults.
- **Dosing**: 100 mg SC at weeks 0, 4, then every 8 weeks. Single-dose prefilled autoinjector.
- **Storage**: refrigerate 2–8 °C; may stay at room temp ≤ 30 days (then discard); do not freeze/shake; allow 30 min to reach room temp before injecting.
- **Key warnings**: serious infections — evaluate for TB before initiation; hypersensitivity.
- **Common AEs**: injection-site reactions (8%: erythema, pain), URI (6%), headache (4%).
- **Device notes**: autoinjector has a viewing window and two audible clicks (start/finish). Failure to actuate or incomplete dose = **product complaint**; injection-site reaction = **AE** (frequent dual AE+PC source).
- **Pregnancy**: no registry; limited human data.

## 4. TRIVOLET® (trivoletine) — oral capsule
- **Class/Indication**: serotonergic antidepressant for major depressive disorder, adults.
- **Dosing**: 20 mg once daily; max 40 mg. Taper to discontinue; do not stop abruptly.
- **Key warnings**: boxed-style warning on suicidality in young adults; serotonin syndrome with other serotonergic agents; discontinuation symptoms.
- **Common AEs**: nausea (11%), insomnia (7%), dry mouth (6%), sexual dysfunction (5%).
- **Pregnancy**: **TRIVOLET Pregnancy Exposure Registry exists** (fictional #: 1-800-555-0142). Third-trimester exposure: risk of neonatal adaptation syndrome. Not recommended during breastfeeding unless benefit outweighs risk.

## 5. BRONVETA® (bronvetarol) — dry-powder inhaler (combination product)
- **Class/Indication**: once-daily maintenance bronchodilator for COPD. **Not a rescue inhaler; not indicated for asthma.**
- **Dosing**: one inhalation daily, same time each day. 30-dose device with dose counter.
- **Device notes**: open cover fully until click; inhale fast and deep; counter decrements per actuation. Counter not decrementing, cover not clicking, powder not released = **product complaint**.
- **Key warnings**: paradoxical bronchospasm; not for acute symptoms.
- **Common AEs**: dry mouth (5%), cough (4%), nasopharyngitis (4%).
